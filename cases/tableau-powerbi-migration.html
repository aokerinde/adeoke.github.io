Retiring Tableau: Migrating 120+ Reports to Power BI at Axxess

I launched Axxess's enterprise BI reporting function in Tableau, building the initial dashboard suite that became the analytics backbone for clinical operations, revenue cycle, and compliance reporting. As Tableau's per-seat licensing costs scaled with every new stakeholder group we onboarded, I led the end-to-end product initiative to migrate the full 120+ report portfolio to Power BI — from the initial business case through final Tableau decommissioning.

01 · Problem Framing

Why Tableau stopped being the right platform

I'd built Axxess's original enterprise BI function on Tableau — the dashboard suite that internal teams and agency customers relied on for clinical operations, revenue cycle, and compliance reporting. But Tableau's per-seat Creator/Explorer licensing meant every new business stakeholder we onboarded to self-service reporting added direct cost, and that cost curve was outpacing the value we were getting relative to Power BI's inclusion in our existing Microsoft/M365 stack.

The product case wasn't "Tableau is bad" — it was a straightforward build-vs-buy-adjacent decision: 120+ reports, a growing user base, and a licensing model misaligned with where the rest of our data and productivity tooling already lived.

02 · Current State

120+ reports, no consolidated ownership model

Before migration planning began, Tableau usage had grown organically for years: report ownership was scattered across teams, naming and versioning weren't standardized, several dashboards held live direct connections to production databases, and licensing costs scaled with headcount rather than usage. There was no single inventory of what existed, who depended on it, or what business criticality each report carried.

03 · Stakeholders

Aligning seven functions around one migration

This wasn't a reporting-tool swap I could run alone — it touched database access, data architecture, custom integrations, and every team consuming the reports. I ran it as a cross-functional product initiative with a clear owner and deliverable per stakeholder group.

04 · Migration Workflow

From license procurement to Tableau decommissioning

I structured the initiative as ten stages, each with a clear owner and exit criteria, so the team always knew what "done" looked like for that phase before moving to the next.

StageOwnerWhat happens
1. Business case & license procurementProduct Manager + FinanceBuild cost-comparison business case (Tableau renewal vs. Power BI), secure budget approval, procure Power BI Pro/Premium licenses
2. Inventory & wave planningProduct Manager + Data AnalystsCatalog all 120+ Tableau reports; tag by usage, business criticality, and complexity; group into a prioritized, wave-based rollout roadmap
3. Governance & security designData Architect + Database ManagerDefine target semantic model standards, row-level security, workspace/naming conventions, and source database access permissions
4. Data pipeline re-engineeringSQL Developers + Data ArchitectRebuild and optimize queries and stored procedures feeding Power BI datasets
5. Custom integration & automationC# DevelopersBuild custom connectors, embedded-analytics integrations, and scheduled refresh/automation tooling
6. Report rebuildData Analysts + DevelopersRecreate dashboards and data models natively in Power BI, wave by wave
7. Data parity validationData Analysts + Data ScientistsReconcile Power BI output against Tableau source; sign off on standard and statistical/predictive visual parity
8. Training & change managementProduct ManagerRun hands-on training, office hours, and a champions network to drive adoption ahead of cutover
9. Wave cutoverProduct Manager + DevelopersGo live per wave, redirect users to Power BI, monitor adoption and performance
10. Tableau license decommissioningProduct Manager + Database ManagerConfirm zero remaining dependency per wave, sunset the corresponding Tableau licenses, and track realized cost savings through to full decommission
05 · Measurable Outcomes

What the migration delivered

MetricResult

User adoption rate94% of active Tableau users transitioned to Power BI

Migration window12 months, license procurement through full cutover
Tableau licensesFully decommissioned — zero seats retained post-migration
Why the decommissioning step mattered: a migration isn't done when the last report is rebuilt — it's done when the licenses you migrated away from stop costing money. Tracking wave-by-wave decommissioning alongside rollout kept the cost-savings case honest instead of letting legacy Tableau seats linger "just in case."
